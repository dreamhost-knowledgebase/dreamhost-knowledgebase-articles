You can use as a new feature in the DreamHost web panel that allows you to copy your staging site to an existing site. A staging instance allows you to test your DreamPress website before it goes live.

The following describes how to copy, password protect, and delete a staging website using the DreamHost web panel.

Creating a staging website

View the following article to add a DreamPress temporary domain.

  • Creating a DreamPress temporary domain

This site can be named anything, but it's recommended you use your domain name followed by dream.press. For example, if your site is 'example.com', create a temporary site named 'example.dream.press'.

After it's created, you'll receive an email with your new username and a link to create a password. You can then log in at example.dream.press/wp-admin.

Using the 'Copy site' option

  1. Navigate to the (Panel > 'DreamPress' > 'Dashboard') page.
  2. Click the Manage button to the right of your staging domain.
  3. Towards the bottom, click the 'Domain' tab.
  4. Click the the 'Copy site' link.
    The Copy site pop-up screen opens prompting you to confirm the destination site:
  5. Select a destination site from the drop-down list (you can also use a Shared site as a staging environment), and then click the yellow 'Start Now' button.
    6. Once your website is finished copying to your staging site, and email is sent to you that confirms the details.

Password-protecting your site

Because a staging site may diminish the SEO ranking of your live site, DreamHost recommends that you password-protect your staging site.

Deleting a staging site

After you set up a staging site, and then decided you'd prefer to use that as your full production site, you can delete the staging instance.
