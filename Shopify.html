Shopify is a site builder that specializes in ecommerce website solutions. You can use it to build your retail website to sell products to your end customers. View more about Shopify at the following link:

  • http://www.shopify.com/about

Pointing your DreamHost site to Shopify

If your domain is registered at DreamHost, you can point your DNS to Shopify.

In the screenshot below, the domain example.comÂ is used. Your domain will show instead when configuring this record.

  1. First, set your domain up in your panel to DNS Only.
  2. Next, view the following link to configure your site at Shopify. This step will give you your Shopify store ID:
    • Configuring your domain
  3. In your DreamHost panel, create an 'A record' to point to Shopify.
    Name (leave blank)
    Type A
    Value 23.227.38.32

  4. Next, create a CNAME record.
    Name www
    Type CNAME
    Value shops.myshopify.com

Your site is now pointed to Shopify. However, only the 'www' version of your site is pointed. The next section explains how to create a redirect to the 'www' version of your site so it always functions.

Redirected your non-www URL to www

  1. View the Redirect Domain articles for detailed instructions on how to create a redirect.
  2. Enter the following fields into your panel:
    • Domain to redirect: example.com
    • Redirect to URL: www.example.com
    Replace example.com with your actual site URL
  3. Save your redirect

Your site now redirects to its 'www' version which then redirects to Shopify.

Point a subdomain to Shopify

If you only want a subdomain such as shop.example.com to point to Shopify, you just need to create a CNAME record as shown above. The 'www' record is technically a subdomain, so you can just change the 'www' to 'shop' or whatever the name of your subdomain is. View the following article for further details:

  • Setting up your subdomain at Shopify
