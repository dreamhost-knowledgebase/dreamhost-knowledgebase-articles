Overview

This article walks you through creating and restoring a backup of your live DreamPress website.

DreamHost automatically creates daily backups of your DreamPress site, which are stored for up to two weeks (four weeks for DreamPress Pro plans). You are also able to create up to ten additional manual backups as needed, which are never removed. If needed, you can restore any of these backups within your panel.

If you recently changed the name of your DreamPress domain, any old backups of the old domain name no longer exist. Only backups of the new name exist. View the following article for further details:

  • Changing your DreamPress domain name

Creating a backup

  1. Navigate to the Manage Websites page.
  2. Click the Manage button to open the Domain Settings page, which allows you to adjust various settings for your site.
    • If in Grid view, click the Manage button at the bottom of the website box.
    • If in List view, click the Manage button at the far right of your domain name.
  3. Under the Manage Your Site section, click the Backups button to the right of Restore Site.
    A list of available backups is displayed.
  4. Click the Create Backup button.
  5. Enter a custom name for the backup (this is optional).
    View the Naming backups section below for naming conventions.

    You may see a warning when creating the backup due to existing automated backups. When you create a new one, the oldest backup is then removed to create room.

You are directed back to the Manage Websites page, where a progress bar displays, showing you the status of the backup being created.

Locking a backup

You can lock/unlock automated backups so they are not automatically overwritten by new daily backups.

Under the Restore Site section, click the 'vertical 3 dots' button to the right of a backup. From the menu, select Lock/Unlock Backup. A lock icon appears next to a backup that is locked.

Restoring a backup

  1. Navigate to the Manage Websites page.
  2. Click the Manage button to open the Domain Settings page, which allows you to adjust various settings for your site.
    • If in Grid view, click the Manage button at the bottom of the website box.
    • If in List view, click the Manage button at the far right of your domain name.
  3. Under the Manage Your Site section, click the Backups button to the right of Restore Site.
    A list of available backups is displayed.
  4. Click the Restore button to the right of the date you want to restore to. A message displays notifying you that this will completely overwrite your existing DreamPress site, and it cannot be undone.
  5. Click the Restore button again to confirm.

You are directed back to the Manage Websites page, where a progress bar displays, showing you the status of the backup restoration. Please wait for the restoration to finish before continuing to work on your website.

What if I do not see this backup option?

If you do not see the option above in your panel, you can always use the following articles to restore your website and database data:

  • How do I restore my website?
  • Restore a database in the panel
 

Naming backups

The names of manual backups must adhere to the following rules:

  • limited to 16 characters
  • alpha-numeric
  • dash ( - )
  • underscore ( _ )
  • Spanish-accented characters are permitted
